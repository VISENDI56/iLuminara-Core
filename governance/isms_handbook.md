## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose
This Information Security Management System (ISMS) Handbook establishes the framework for managing information security within iLuminara Health Systems. It defines the policies, procedures, and controls necessary to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets supporting the FRENASA platform.

### 1.2 Scope
The ISMS applies to:
- All information systems and data processing facilities
- All personnel, contractors, and third parties
- All locations where iLuminara conducts business
- All information assets, including health data, intellectual property, and operational data

### 1.3 ISMS Objectives
- Achieve and maintain ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certification
- Protect patient health information and privacy
- Ensure business continuity and resilience
- Maintain regulatory compliance across jurisdictions
- Foster a security-aware culture

## 2. Information Security Policy

### 2.1 Policy Statement
iLuminara is committed to protecting its information assets through the implementation of an effective Information Security Management System. All personnel are required to comply with this policy and associated procedures.

### 2.2 Policy Principles
- **Confidentiality**: Information is accessible only to authorized individuals
- **Integrity**: Information is accurate and complete
- **Availability**: Information is accessible when needed
- **Accountability**: Actions are traceable to responsible individuals
- **Compliance**: All activities comply with applicable laws and regulations

## 3. Organization of Information Security

### 3.1 Governance Structure
- **Board of Directors**: Ultimate responsibility for information security
- **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)**: Executive leadership
- **Information Security Committee**: Strategic oversight
- **Data Protection Officer**: Privacy compliance
- **Security Operations Center (SOC)**: Operational monitoring

### 3.2 Roles and Responsibilities

#### 3.2.1 All Personnel
- Comply with information security policies and procedures
- Report security incidents promptly
- Protect information assets in their care
- Complete required security training

#### 3.2.2 System Owners
- Define security requirements for their systems
- Ensure appropriate controls are implemented
- Coordinate with security teams for risk assessments
- Approve changes to their systems

#### 3.2.3 Information Security Team
- Implement and maintain security controls
- Monitor security posture
- Respond to security incidents
- Provide security awareness training

## 4. Asset Management

### 4.1 Asset Inventory
All information assets must be inventoried and classified according to their sensitivity and criticality. The asset inventory includes:
- Hardware assets (servers, workstations, mobile devices)
- Software assets (applications, operating systems)
- Data assets (health records, intellectual property)
- Network assets (routers, switches, firewalls)

### 4.2 Asset Classification
Assets are classified as:
- **Public**: Information that can be freely disclosed
- **Internal**: Information for internal use only
- **Confidential**: Information requiring protection
- **Restricted**: Highly sensitive information with severe impact if disclosed

### 4.3 Asset Handling
- Assets must be labeled according to their classification
- Access controls must be implemented based on classification
- Assets must be securely disposed of when no longer needed

## 5. Access Control

### 5.1 Access Control Policy
Access to information systems and data is granted based on:
- Business need
- Least privilege principle
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Regular access reviews

### 5.2 User Access Management
- User accounts are created only for authorized individuals
- Access rights are approved by system owners
- Access is revoked promptly when no longer needed
- Shared accounts are prohibited

### 5.3 User Responsibilities
- Users must protect their credentials
- Users must not share access with others
- Users must report lost or compromised credentials immediately
- Users must comply with acceptable use policies

## 6. Physical and Environmental Security

### 6.1 Secure Areas
- Server rooms and data centers are secured with access controls
- Visitor access is logged and escorted
- Clean desk policy is enforced
- Equipment is protected from environmental hazards

### 6.2 Equipment Security
- Equipment is secured against unauthorized access
- Mobile devices are encrypted and protected
- Equipment is inventoried and tracked
- Equipment disposal follows secure procedures

## 7. Operations Security

### 7.1 Operational Procedures
- Change management procedures are followed
- Backup and recovery procedures are tested regularly
- Capacity management ensures system availability
- Incident management procedures are established

### 7.2 Malware Protection
- Antivirus software is deployed on all systems
- Regular malware scans are performed
- Suspicious activities are monitored and investigated
- Incident response procedures are followed for malware incidents

### 7.3 Backup and Recovery
- Critical data is backed up regularly
- Backup media is stored securely offsite
- Backup integrity is tested periodically
- Recovery procedures are documented and tested

## 8. Communications Security

### 8.1 Network Security
- Networks are segmented based on security requirements
- Firewalls and intrusion detection systems are deployed
- Remote access is secured with VPNs
- Network traffic is monitored for anomalies

### 8.2 Information Transfer
- Information is encrypted during transfer
- Secure protocols (HTTPS, SFTP) are used
- Data loss prevention controls are implemented
- Information transfer agreements are established

## 9. System Acquisition, Development and Maintenance

### 9.1 Security Requirements
- Security requirements are defined for all systems
- Security controls are implemented during development
- Security testing is performed before deployment
- Security patches are applied promptly

### 9.2 Secure Development
- Secure coding practices are followed
- Code reviews include security assessments
- Static and dynamic analysis tools are used
- Security testing is integrated into development lifecycle

### 9.3 Supplier Relationships
- Suppliers are assessed for security capabilities
- Security requirements are included in contracts
- Supplier performance is monitored
- Supplier access is controlled

## 10. Incident Management

### 10.1 Incident Response
- Security incidents are reported immediately
- Incident response team is activated
- Incident containment, eradication, and recovery are performed
- Lessons learned are documented

### 10.2 Incident Classification
Incidents are classified as:
- **Low**: Minor impact, contained quickly
- **Medium**: Moderate impact, requires investigation
- **High**: Significant impact, requires immediate response
- **Critical**: Severe impact, business continuity threatened

### 10.3 Communication
- Stakeholders are informed according to incident severity
- Regulatory reporting requirements are met
- Public communication is coordinated when necessary

## 11. Compliance

### 11.1 Legal and Regulatory Requirements
- Compliance with applicable laws and regulations
- Regular compliance assessments
- Audit findings are addressed promptly
- Compliance status is reported to management

### 11.2 Internal Compliance
- Internal policies and procedures are followed
- Compliance monitoring is performed
- Non-compliance is addressed through corrective actions
- Compliance training is provided

## 12. Human Resource Security

### 12.1 Personnel Security
- Background checks are performed for sensitive positions
- Security responsibilities are included in job descriptions
- Security training is provided to all personnel
- Termination procedures include access revocation

### 12.2 Awareness and Training
- Security awareness training is mandatory
- Role-specific training is provided
- Training effectiveness is measured
- Refresher training is conducted annually

## 13. Business Continuity Management

### 13.1 Business Impact Analysis
- Critical business processes are identified
- Impact of disruptions is assessed
- Recovery time objectives are defined
- Recovery strategies are developed

### 13.2 Continuity Planning
- Business continuity plans are developed
- Plans are tested regularly
- Contact lists are maintained and updated
- Alternate work arrangements are established

## 14. Monitoring and Measurement

### 14.1 Security Metrics
- Security incidents and trends
- Compliance with policies and procedures
- Effectiveness of security controls
- Security awareness training completion

### 14.2 Internal Audits
- Internal audits are conducted annually
- Audit findings are documented
- Corrective actions are implemented
- Audit results are reported to management

### 14.3 Management Review
- ISMS performance is reviewed quarterly
- Management review inputs include audit results, metrics, and incident reports
- Improvement opportunities are identified
- Resources for improvement are allocated

## 15. Continual Improvement

### 15.1 Corrective and Preventive Actions
- Non-conformities are identified and analyzed
- Root causes are determined
- Corrective actions are implemented
- Effectiveness of actions is verified

### 15.2 Security Improvements
- New threats and vulnerabilities are monitored
- Security controls are enhanced as needed
- Technology updates are evaluated
- Best practices are adopted

## 16. Document Control

### 16.1 Document Management
- All ISMS documents are controlled
- Document changes are approved
- Current versions are available to authorized personnel
- Obsolete documents are removed

### 16.2 Record Management
- Security records are maintained
- Records are protected and retained appropriately
- Records are available for audits and reviews
